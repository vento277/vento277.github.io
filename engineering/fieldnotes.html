Richard Feynman (1918 ~ 1988)

Richard Feynman was a renowned American theoretical physicist, best known for his contributions to the development of quantum mechanics and quantum electrodynamics (QED).

Beyond his scientific work, Feynman was also famous for his ability to explain complex topics in an engaging and accessible way. His book, "The Feynman Lectures on Physics", are widely enjoyed by both scientists and non-scientists alike.
Feynman Technique is a popularized learning method that involves four steps:
  1. Select a concept and study it.
  2. Teach the concept to someone else (or yourself).
  3. Identify gaps in your understanding and refine your knowledge.
  4. Repeat steps 2~3 until you get the hang of the topic.
